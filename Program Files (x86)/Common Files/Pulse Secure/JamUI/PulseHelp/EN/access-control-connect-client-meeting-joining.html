Access Pulse Secure client Collaboration Meeting Functions

If you have a Pulse Secure client connection to a server that has been enabled as a Pulse Collaboration Suite server, you can access Pulse Collaboration Suite functions from the Pulse Secure client tray icon and join a meeting, schedule a meeting, or exit from a meeting. Alternatively, you can log in to the Pulse Secure client server through the Web portal and then click the Meetings button to access Pulse Collaboration features.

To access Pulse Collaboration options:

  1. Right-click the Pulse Secure client icon in the Windows system tray to display the pop-up menu.
  2. Click Pulse Collaboration, the meeting server, and then click Start Meeting.
  3. If prompted, provide your login credentials. If there is a scheduled meeting for this time, Pulse Secure client opens a browser window that displays a Start Meeting button. Otherwise, view the meeting schedule. Select the meeting in the meeting schedule, and then click Start Meeting to open the Pulse Collaboration Suite client and join the meeting. When you join a Pulse Collaboration meeting, the Pulse Collaboration client downloads and opens. For details on how to use Pulse Collaboration Suite, click the Help link within the Pulse Collaboration interface.

    If your administrator has enabled Microsoft Outlook integration, click the link to download and install the Microsoft Outlook plug-in. The plug-in allows you to schedule meetings from within Outlook and send invitations to attendees using Outlook e-mail.

    Click Schedule Meeting to schedule a meeting and invite attendees.

    Click Instant Meeting or Support Meeting (if your administrator has enabled these meeting types) as needed. An instant meeting is similar to a standard meeting. It allows you to quickly create a meeting by automatically applying your role-level settings, such as authentication requirements, remote control, and secure chatting. A support meeting is a two-person meeting that is primarily intended to allow online troubleshooting. A support meeting allows desktop sharing and remote control operations.

    The Meetings button at the top of the browser window returns you to the meeting schedule from anywhere within the Web portal.

    For more information about Pulse Collaboration features, click the Help button in the browser window.

To disconnect from a meeting:

  1. Click Pulse Collaboration and the meeting server, and then click Sign Out.

    Using the tray icon sign out menu item accomplishes the same thing as signing out through the Pulse Collaboration client interface.
